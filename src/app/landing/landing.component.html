DeChat

A simple chat that uses SOLID to decentralize the data as much as possible. With this chat you are the owner of your information.

Login and access to the app Documentation

You need a POD server provider in order to use the chat. You can register to get one in Solid Community or via Inrupt. There are more ways, even you can create your own Solid server.

SOLID

Solid was created by the creator of the World Wide Web, Sir Tim Berners-Lee. Its mission is to reshape the web as we know it. Solid pretends to decentralize the data from the applications.

Centralized Web

The first web browser was also an editor. The idea was pretending to allow everyone to read and modify content on the web.

However, when the first browser was popularized, called Mosaic, it included multimedia and editing was taken out. It was considered a too difficult problem. This change was the first curtailing of the web’s promise and spawned an effort led by Tim and others to get the functionality back. It was dubbed the ‘read-write web’ and lead to Richard McManus' seminal article published in 2003.

Recentralizing the Web

Solid has taken 15 years of development to finally deliver this.

The issue with writing data, as Wikipedia and others have learned, it's that you need a certain control over who can write and edit contents. That means you need to have permissions - what can individuals do to the data. And to be able to do this you need to have an identity system - a way to ensure that an individual is who it purport to be.

Linked Data

The web was always designed to be a space for data as well as files. The Linked Data Platform group took this further by creating a standard to work with file systems. This combined with the Unix philosophy provide a powerful way to achieve the goal.

The read-write capability and the management of data are the key of the original and strong concept of the web. They were unfortunately lost along the way as the web evolved.
